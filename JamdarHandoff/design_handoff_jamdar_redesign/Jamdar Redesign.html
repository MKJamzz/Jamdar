Jamdar · Desktop · UI Redesign

A music finder that feels like a player, not a downloader.

The redesign trades the cramped dark utility for a bright, soft, premium surface inspired by slush.app — warm paper, generously rounded cards, and a single coral accent doing the heavy lifting. Search is the hero up top, results breathe in the center, a proper dark Now Playing dock anchors the bottom, and the download queue slides in from the right only when you need it. Platform colors (YouTube red, SoundCloud orange) survive as small accent moments — a thumbnail glow, a badge, a source label — never as visual clutter.

02 — Component notes
Three components do most of the work
Result card

Rich, scannable rows

  • 80px rounded cover with a platform-tinted glow + corner badge — the constraint-required YT/SC mark, always visible.
  • Title in display type, then a muted line: source label · uploader · mono duration.
  • Two clear actions — outline Preview, coral Download — plus a quiet "open in browser" glyph.
  • The previewing card gets a coral ring + live equalizer so you never lose your place.
Now Playing dock

A real player, not a strip

  • Dark indigo-ink bar spans the window base — premium weight, lets coral pop.
  • 60px art + title + source dot left; big coral play center between restart/stop.
  • Waveform scrubber with mono time codes and a PREVIEW tag for the 30s clip.
  • Volume + a one-tap Download this on the right — preview to save in one move.
Queue row

Status at a glance

  • Cover + title + format chip (MP3·320 / FLAC) in mono; cancel ✕ always reachable.
  • Coral progress bar + % always visible while active — a hard requirement, honored.
  • State colors the whole row: green "Saved to drive", red "unavailable" with Retry.
  • Lives in a dockable slide-out; the titlebar badge shows the active count when collapsed.
